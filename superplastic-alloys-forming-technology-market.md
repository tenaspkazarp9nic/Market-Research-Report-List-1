<p><ul><li>Microstructural Superplasticity</li><li>Dynamic Superplasticity</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Superplastic alloys forming technology encompasses two primary market types: microstructural superplasticity and dynamic superplasticity. Microstructural superplasticity refers to the deformation of fine-grained materials at elevated temperatures, allowing significant elongation without fracture. In contrast, dynamic superplasticity occurs at lower temperatures under high strain rates, enabling rapid forming of complex shapes. Both types leverage the unique properties of superplastic alloys, enhancing manufacturing efficiency and product quality, making them vital for industries like aerospace and automotive where precision and performance are critical.</p></p>
